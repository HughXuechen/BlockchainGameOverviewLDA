A Game for Task Offloading in Reputation-Based Consortium Blockchain Networks

Die Wang , Graduate Student Member, IEEE, Yunjian Jia , Member, IEEE, Liang Liang ,

Abstract—This letter studies the task offloading in reputation-
based consortium blockchain networks, where the tasks are
transmitted to Edge Computing Servers (ECSs) due to limited
resources. We propose a novel Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS)
consensus mechanism in which validation nodes (including active
nodes and backup nodes) are voted based on their reputation.
The incentive identifies the reputation and the consensus delay
as two major factors determining the reward. A three-stage
Stackelberg game is developed to jointly minimize cost of the
users and maximize utilities of the master node and the valida-
tion nodes. We analyze the unique Stackelberg equilibrium exists
in the proposed game by the backward induction. The simula-
tion results demonstrate that the designed incentive is feasible for
trust management, and the proposed consensus has lower delay
and higher decentralization compared with the traditional DPoS.
Index Terms—Consortium blockchain, reputation, task offload-
ing, edge computing, Stackelberg game.
I. INTRODUCTION
RECENTLY, mobile edge computing (MEC) has been
proposed to deal with computation-intense tasks for
mobile devices [1]. MEC enables mobile devices to offload
tasks to nearby Edge Computing Servers (ECSs), which
can both reduce delay and energy consumption compared
with centralized cloud. However, there are many security
and privacy issues for interaction between entities in MEC.
Indeed, the architecture may be suffered data leakage and
single point of failure. In addition, the unauthorized entity
may achieve malicious access to utilize edge computing
resources. Blockchain is considered as a promising technology
that ensures secure interaction with untrusted nodes without
trust-dependent third parties [2].

Driven by the above-mentioned analysis, integrating
blockchain technology with MEC has attracted increas-
ing attention from researchers and developers. In [3], the
blockchain-based vehicular ad hoc networks (VANETs) is
studied to ensure secure computation offloading under access
control by smart contracts. In [4], the blockchain-based MEC
framework is studied to complete adaptive resource allocation
and task offloading. In [5], the multilayer blockchain archi-
tecture based three-valued subjective logic theory is studied to
enhance trustworthy management and resist launching attacks.
In [6], the blockchain enabled multi-side offloading scheme is
studied to ensure mobility privacy under deadline and resource
constraints. In [7], the blockchain-enabled MEC framework is
studied to achieve the optimal trade-off between the consen-
sus delay of blockchain system and the energy consumption
of MEC system. In [8], the blockchain-based MEC architec-
ture for cooperative hospital networks is studied to offload and
share health data without privacy leakage. However, most of
the above literatures only focus on enhancing system security
by access control for smart contracts ignoring how to ensure
the trustworthiness of computing resources (i.e., throughput)
and using the traditional consensus mechanism in blockchain-
enabled MEC networks leading to high delay cost with low
decentralization. In addition, most studies consider the overall
system performance only without splitting and distributing the
utilities among various types of entities.
In this letter, we study the problem of task offloading in
consortium-based blockchain assisted MEC networks. We pro-
pose an improved Delegated Proof of Stake (DPoS) consensus
scheme including the voting mechanism and the propagation
mechanism. Due to the complex coupling relationship in the
blockchain-based MEC networks, we model the task offload-
ing among the users, master node, and validation nodes as
a Stackelberg game. We prove a unique Stackelberg equilib-
rium exists in the proposed game by the backward induction.
Simulation results demonstrate that the effectiveness of the
designed incentive and the performance gains of the improved
consensus over the traditional DPoS (i.e., EoS) [9].

V. CONCLUSION
In this letter, we have investigated the problem of task
offloading in consortium-based blockchain assisted mobile
edge computing networks where the reputation and the incen-
tive for improved DPoS consensus are considered. Specifically,
we model the task offloading among the users, master node,
and validation nodes as a three-stage Stackelberg game. We
prove a unique Stackelberg equilibrium exists in the proposed
game. The simulation results demonstrate that the designed
incentive is feasible for trust management and the improved
consensus has lower delay and higher decentralization com-
pared with the traditional DPoS.
